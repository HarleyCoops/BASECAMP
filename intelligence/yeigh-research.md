# Frank Yeigh (1860-1935) -- Archival Research Report

## Biographical Summary (from Dictionary of Canadian Biography, Vol. XVI)

**Full name:** Frank Yeigh
**Born:** July 21, 1860, Burford Township, Upper Canada (note: some sources say 1861; the DCB uses 1860)
**Died:** October 2, 1935, Toronto, Ontario
**Buried:** Mount Pleasant Cemetery, Toronto
**Parents:** Edmund Lossing Yeigh and Adaliza (Ada) Whitehead (Pennsylvania German and Scottish ancestry)
**First wife:** Kate Eva Westlake (m. Oct 27, 1892; d. 1906) -- no children
**Second wife:** Annie Louise Laird (m. Sept 2, 1908, Kingston, ON) -- one son. Annie was a schoolteacher and friend of ACC founder Elizabeth Parker Fulton. They met at the 1906 Yoho camp.
**Occupations:** Printer, publisher, writer, lecturer, publicist, civil servant
**Address (c. 1907):** 667 Spadina Avenue, Toronto

DCB author: Terry Crowley. Full entry at `https://www.biographi.ca/en/bio/yeigh_frank_16E.html`

## 1. PHOTOGRAPHS AT THE 1906 ACC CAMP

**Finding: Yeigh is NOT credited as photographer in CAJ Vol. 1, No. 1.**

The article "Canada's First Alpine Club Camp" (CAJ 1:1, pp. 47-57) is BY Yeigh, but the photo credits within the article are:
- "Photo, Rev. G. R. Kinney" -- Mount Burgess and Emerald Lake
- "Photo, Byron Harmon" -- The Dining Pavilion at Yoho Camp
- "Photo, F. W. Freeborn" -- Mount Vice-President, The Official Climb; Residence Park, Yoho Camp

Two additional plates ("Our Bivouacs on the Yoho Trail," "The Men in Buckskin," "Two Veterans") appear without explicit photographer credit. These COULD be Yeigh's, but there is no attribution.

The header in the existing repo file (`C:\Users\chris\ELL\BASECAMP\articles\06-first-camp-yeigh.md`, line 5) reads: *"Photos by Geo. R. B. Kinney, Byron Harmon, Frank W. Freeborn, and perhaps Frank Yeigh"* -- the "perhaps" is editorial speculation, not confirmed by the journal itself.

**His book "Through the Heart of Canada" (1910/1911) contains 38 black-and-white photographs** of his cross-Canada journey including the Rockies, Selkirks, and Kootenays. These could include images from the 1906 trip. The book is digitized on Canadiana.ca.

**Status: NO confirmed Yeigh photographs from the 1906 camp. Possible unattributed plates. Check "Through the Heart of Canada" for reused images.**

## 5. ACC CONNECTION -- OFFICER ROLES AND SUBSEQUENT CAMPS

### Membership Status (1907)
- Listed in CAJ Vol. 1 members list as **Graduating Member** (not Active -- he had not yet completed the graduation climb)
- Address: 667 Spadina Ave., Toronto, Ontario
- Listed as "Yeigh, F."

### Officer Positions
- **Advisory Committee:** 1908-1912
- **Chairman, Toronto Section:** 1914
- **Founded Toronto Section gatherings:** In 1908, the first recorded gathering of ACC members in Toronto was held at Yeigh's home on Spadina Avenue

### Promotional Work
- Wrote frequent articles in The Toronto Star promoting the ACC
- Donated a copy of *Through the Heart of Canada* to the ACC library
- Described in the CAJ as "thorough but not ambitious"

### Marriage Connection
- Met Annie Louise Laird at the 1906 camp (she was a friend of Elizabeth Parker Fulton, the ACC founder)
- Married her in 1908; her inheritance allowed him to leave civil service

### Subsequent Camps
- The DCB biography confirms he was an ACC member and hiked in Yoho National Park "after 1906," but does not specify which subsequent camps he attended. The Advisory Committee role (1908-1912) suggests ongoing involvement.

## 6. ADDITIONAL FINDINGS

### Letter to John Muir (1895)
- **Date:** July 3, 1895
- **Archive:** University of the Pacific, Holt-Atherton Special Collections (Reel 08, Image 1073)
- **Content:** Yeigh asks Muir about glacier sizes and requests bibliography on glaciers for Ontario's Director of Mines
- **URL:** [Scholarly Commons, UOP](https://scholarlycommons.pacific.edu/jmcl/961/)
- **Significance:** Shows Yeigh's interest in glaciers predates the ACC by over a decade

### Save the Children Fund (Canada)
- Yeigh helped establish the Canadian committee in Toronto (1922)
- Became organizing secretary in 1925
- Published articles on war-affected children
- Annie continued the work after his death (1935); it became a separate Canadian body in 1946
- Academic thesis on this topic at University of Guelph: "How the Dominion Heard the Cry: The Early History of the Canadian Save the Children Fund, 1922-1946"

### Pauline Johnson Connection
- In 1892, as president of the Young Men's Liberal Club, Yeigh invited E. Pauline Johnson to recite at a Toronto literary evening -- this was her breakthrough public appearance
- Served as her publicist ("Yeigh-man") until 1894
- The LAC fonds is specifically about this relationship

### Copyright Status
- Yeigh died October 2, 1935
- Under Canadian law (pre-2022 rule): life + 50 years = **public domain since January 1, 1986**
- All his published works are in the public domain in Canada
