# George Vaux Jr.

**Vital dates:** 1863–1927  
**Affiliation:** Philadelphia Quaker family; amateur glaciologist and mountain photographer  
**Major expedition:** Alpine Club of Canada camp, Yoho Pass (July 8–16, 1906)

## Role at 1906 Yoho camp
George Vaux Jr. and his younger brother William S. Vaux Jr. attended the ACC camp at Yoho Pass as amateur glaciologists and photographers. Together with their sister Mary Vaux (who assisted with photograph hand-coloring and preservation), they undertook systematic glacier observation and repeat photography across the Canadian Rockies from 1897 to 1913.

## Bio hook
One of two Quaker brothers from Philadelphia who became pioneers of Canadian glacier photography and monitoring. Through repeat photography sequences of Illecillewaet, Yoho, and Asulkan glaciers (1897–1913), the Vaux brothers established the methodological foundation for modern Canadian glacier monitoring, making their 1906 Yoho expedition a key moment in the history of glaciology and mountain science in Canada.
