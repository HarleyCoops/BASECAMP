# Édouard Feuz Sr.

**Birth–Death:** 1859–1944  
**Nationality:** Swiss (Grindelwald, Canton Bern)  
**Role at 1906 Yoho Camp:** Lead mountain guide for Alpine Club of Canada camp; likely guide for Vice-President climb, July 10, 1906

## Bio Hook
Édouard Feuz Sr. was the patriarch of the legendary Feuz family mountain guides who transplanted Swiss alpine traditions to the Canadian Rockies. Hired by the Canadian Pacific Railway in 1899, he brought professional guide expertise to early 1900s mountaineering expeditions. At the 1906 Alpine Club of Canada Yoho Pass camp, he led major ascents including the Vice-President climb on July 10. His three sons—Édouard Jr., Ernest, and Walter—became celebrated Rockies guides in their own right, extending the family's mountaineering legacy through the 20th century.
