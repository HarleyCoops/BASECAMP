# Arthur Oliver Wheeler (1860-1945)

## Role at 1906 Yoho Camp
Principal organizer, first president of the Alpine Club of Canada (founded 1906), and key scientific advisor for the club's inaugural camp at Yoho Pass, July 8-16, 1906. Wheeler directed camp logistics, organized mountaineering activities, and conducted photo-topographic survey work and glacier measurements on the Yoho Glacier during the camp.

## Bio Hook
Arthur Oliver Wheeler was an Irish-born (1860) Canadian surveyor and mountaineer who arrived in Canada in 1876 and became one of the most influential figures in Rocky Mountain exploration and scientific documentation. As a Dominion Land Surveyor (DLS) and appointed Topographer of the Department of the Interior, he pioneered photo-topographic survey methods in the Canadian Rockies alongside J.J. McArthur. Wheeler co-founded the Alpine Club of Canada in 1906 (first president 1906-1910) and served as editor of the Canadian Alpine Journal (1907-1930). In 1913, he was appointed Boundary Commissioner to survey the Alberta-British Columbia interprovincial boundary, and led the landmark 1911 Jasper Park expedition with photographer Byron Harmon. Wheeler died in 1945 and was later designated a National Historic Person by Parks Canada.

## Fonds & Archives Located

### High Priority (Primary 1906 Documentation)

1. **Whyte Museum of the Canadian Rockies, Banff, AB**
   - Fonds: M546 / V771 (Arthur Oliver Wheeler fonds)
   - URL: https://archives.whyte.org/en/list?q=name:%22Wheeler,+Arthur+Oliver%22&p=1&ps=&sort=title_sort+asc
   - Contents: 53 diaries (1885-1944), 2 scrapbooks (1902-1940), 7 volumes field notes, 66 photograph albums (~5,000 images)
   - Status: No online digitization; on-site consultation required
   - **1906 Relevance:** Diaries span the entire 1906 camp period; likely contain daily camp observations and personal reflections. Photograph albums from survey period overlap temporally.

2. **Alpine Club of Canada Archives**
   - Main location: Whyte Museum of the Canadian Rockies
   - Secondary location: University of Calgary Archives & Special Collections (Calgary Section only)
   - URL: https://searcharchives.ucalgary.ca/alpine-club-of-canada-2
   - Contents: 1399+ items including 1906 Yoho camp records (minutes, participant lists, photographs, equipment records, trip reports)
   - Status: Not fully digitized; on-site consultation required
   - **1906 Relevance:** Official camp documentation, including Wheeler's organizational records and camp reports published in Canadian Alpine Journal Vol.1 (1907).

3. **Internet Archive / Wikisource (Public Domain - FREELY ACCESSIBLE)**
   - Canadian Alpine Journal, Vol. 1 (1907-08)
   - URL: https://archive.org/details/canadianalpinejo01alpiuoft
   - Downloaded: 26 MB PDF
   - Contents: Complete journal including:
     - "Canada's First Alpine Club Camp" (official 1906 camp report, pp. 1-15)
     - "The Canadian Rockies: a field for an Alpine Club" (Wheeler's foundational article)
     - "Observations of the Yoho Glacier" (Wheeler, pp. 149-159)
     - "Wapta Icefield" topographic map accompanying glacier observations
   - **1906 Relevance:** Primary published documentation of the camp with Wheeler's scientific findings and observations.

   - Status: Partial digital access; full 60,000-plate collection at LAC
   - **1906 Relevance:** Archive covers Wheeler's systematic mountain photography; 1906 Yoho valley plates likely catalogued but not yet directly identified in searches.
