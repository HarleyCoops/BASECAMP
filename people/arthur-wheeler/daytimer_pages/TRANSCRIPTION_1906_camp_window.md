## What this transcription proves

1. **Opening day reconnaissance:** The first climb of Mt. Burgess on July 8 was a trail-scouting mission by the three lead guides (Edward Feuz, Christian Feuz, Dr. Herdman), not a recreational summit.
2. **Wheeler's logistical role:** He was managing pony transport, luggage, arrivals, account settlement, and was not on the formal climbs — consistent with his role as camp director.
3. **Vaux glacier survey IS documented in a primary source:** July 15 entry places Wheeler at Yoho Glacier with the Vaux brothers, setting out plates, marking rocks, photographing, taking instrumental readings. This is the field day for the glaciology program that became the Vaux family's multi-decade glacier monitoring project.
4. **A.P. Coleman's ice-axe sale on July 13** raised over $75 — potential first ACC fundraiser. Worth cross-checking against the CAJ Vol. 1 No. 1 article and the ACC treasury records.
5. **Separate "Vaux Camp"** existed adjacent to the main Yoho camp — a distinct encampment for the Vaux family's glaciology operations.
6. **Elizabeth Parker stayed last** — she and her party remained through July 17, the only attendees on the final dismantling day.
7. **Routine daily climbs:** Mt. Stephen (Jul 10), Mt. President (Jul 10), Mt. Wapta (Jul 11, 12, 13 — three separate ascents), Mt. Field (Jul 12), Lookout Pt. (Jul 12), Mt. Marpole (Jul 16).
