# Julia Wilmotte Henshaw (1869-1937)

## Role at 1906 Yoho Camp
Botanist and mountaineer at the inaugural Alpine Club of Canada camp at Yoho Pass (1906). Published "The Mountain Wildflowers of Western Canada" as both a standalone book and as the first article in the Canadian Alpine Journal, Vol. 1, No. 1 (1906). One of the first women members of the Alpine Club of Canada and accomplished first ascents.

## Bio Hook
Vancouver-based journalist, alpine botanist, and explorer who bridged the 19th and 20th centuries as a pioneering woman in mountaineering. After her botanical work with the ACC in the Rocky Mountains (1900s-1910s), she served as a WWI Red Cross ambulance driver in France, earning the Chevalier of the Legion of Honour and becoming a fellow of the Royal Canadian Geographic Society.

## 1906 Yoho Camp Artifacts Found

### Direct Evidence
- Whyte Museum M67/V225: Henshaw's annotated field copy of "Wild Flowers of the North American Mountains" with dated observations marking specimen collection locations. This is her personal working copy from the field period, directly documenting species identification and locations.

### Photographic Evidence (1910-11, Selkirk Mountains)
- Whyte Museum V653/NG-909, V653/NG-908, V653/NG-907: Three glass negatives showing Henshaw on mountain trails with Mary Schaffer, Selkirk Mountains. Documented subjects: "On tote road, Glacier, B.C., Mrs. Henshaw & horse" and "Trail to Loans Glacier with pony and figures." While these are 1910-11 (not 1906), they document her continued Alpine work and partnership with the pioneering mountaineer Mary Schaffer.

### Published Works
- "The Mountain Wildflowers of Western Canada" — Published as book (1906) and CAJ Vol. 1, No. 1 (1906). Whyte has her annotated personal copy in Lillian Gest fonds. Out-of-copyright full-text scans potentially available via Internet Archive.
- "Wild Flowers of the North American Mountains" (1915 edition)
- Postcard series "Rocky Mountain Wildflowers" — Commercial publication by Raphael Tuck & Sons, London

## Key Findings

**Confirmed Holdings:** Whyte Museum is the major institutional repository with 31 records spanning photographs, annotated field copies, postcards, and secondary sources. The most significant artifact is Henshaw's personal annotated working copy of her wildflowers book in the Lillian Gest fonds, which documents specific specimen locations with field notes — a direct link to her 1906 collecting work.

**Access Challenge:** The primary 1906 Yoho camp documentation (group photographs, participant lists, camp journals) is held in the Alpine Club of Canada fonds at Whyte, but digital surrogates are not online. In-person archival consultation or staff-assisted requests required.

**Secondary but Valuable:** Glass plate negatives of Henshaw mountaineering in the Selkirks (1910-11) with Mary Schaffer document her continuing botanical and exploratory work, though dated after the 1906 camp.

**Digital Access:** Henshaw's published wildflower books (1906, 1915) are candidates for full-text digitization at Internet Archive as out-of-copyright works; direct search recommended.
