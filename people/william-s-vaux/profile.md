# William S. Vaux Jr.

**Vital dates:** 1872–1908  
**Affiliation:** Philadelphia Quaker family; amateur glaciologist and mountain photographer  
**Major expedition:** Alpine Club of Canada camp, Yoho Pass (July 8–16, 1906)  
**Note:** Died at age 36; limited documented career

## Role at 1906 Yoho camp
William S. Vaux Jr. attended the ACC camp at Yoho Pass as co-photographer and glacier observer alongside his older brother George Vaux Jr. The brothers undertook systematic glacier observation and repeat photography across the Canadian Rockies. The 1906 expedition at Yoho Pass represents one of the documented collaborative expeditions before William's death in 1908.

## Bio hook
Younger brother in a pioneering glacier photography team. Though his life was cut short at 36, William S. Vaux Jr.'s contributions to the 1906 Yoho expedition and the broader repeat photography project (1897–1913) established protocols for Canadian glacier monitoring that outlasted him, securing the Vaux brothers' legacy in the history of glaciology.
