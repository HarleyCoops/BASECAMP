02 / The approach march

Railway arrival becomes a 10-mile ascent through today’s Yoho.

On July 9, 1906 the party crossed the Kicking Horse, followed the forest road to Emerald Lake, crossed the glacial delta, then climbed the steep wall toward Yoho Pass. The modern visitor can still read the route in roads, trails, deltas, junctions, and trail signs.

07 / Route engine

Five journeys, one camp as the hub.

1906-07-09 · 10 mi · +1925 ftField Station to Camp Yoho (1906 approach march)

The march of July 9, 1906. Crossed Kicking-Horse bridge, 7-mile forest road to Emerald Lake Chalet, traverse of northern glacial delta with stream crossings, 1000-ft cliff ascent through spruce forest to Yoho Pass summit.

1906-07-10 to 1906-07-14 (5 official ascents) · 5 mi · +4050 ftOfficial Climb of The Vice-President

From camp, trail to Yoho Pass summit, right to Michael Peak shoulder near timber-line, long rock slide, steep snow slopes to arete, knife-edge arete to Angle Peak, snowfield walk to summit.

4 consecutive trips during camp week · 30 mi · +2000 ftYoho Valley Round Trip (2-day)

Lower trail past Takakkaw Falls and Laughing Falls to Twin Falls and Yoho Glacier, overnight at Laughing Falls camp, return by upper trail with views of entire 15-mile valley.

3 trips during camp week · 4 mi · +2000 ftEmerald Glacier Trip

From camp, partly same route as Upper Yoho trail to Inspiration Point, then left turn toward glacier foot. Stiff ascent over boulders. Snow line at ~8000 ft. Ice-step climbing to main icefield with seracs and crevasses.

1906-07-16 (many campers used this return) · 8 mi · +1000 ftBurgess Pass Return Route

Return from camp to Mt. Stephen House via Burgess Pass trail. Views of President Range, Emerald mountains, glaciers, Emerald Lake below.

08 / The week

The camp can be told as a timed expedition.

07-08

Campers arrive at Field Station. Route-scouting party departs for Vice-President.

07-09

Camp opens. March from Field: bridge, 7-mile road, Emerald Lake Chalet, delta crossing, cliff ascent to Yoho Pass.

07-10

First official climb of Vice-President. 9 graduate (first: Miss McLennan, Miss Hobbs). Burgess also climbed.

07-11

Second official climb. 9 graduate. Wapta climbed.

07-12

Third official climb. 13 graduate. Collie, Field, Wapta climbed.

07-13

Fourth official climb. 7 graduate. Wapta climbed.

07-14

Fifth official climb. 4 graduate. President summit added. Yoho Glacier observation begins.

07-15

Yoho Glacier observation continues. Daily trips to Inspiration Point.

07-16

Camp breaks up. Marpole and Amgadamo first ascents. Many return via Burgess Pass.

07-17

Pack out continues.

The timeline gives the live presentation a pacing track: map, image, person, climb, glacier, return.
