# Frank Yeigh (1861–1935)
## Toronto Journalist, Author, Publicist & Alpine Club Charter Member

---

## Overview

Frank Yeigh was a prominent Ontario public servant, journalist, and author whose prolific career spanned four decades (1890s–1930s). A charter member of the Alpine Club of Canada (1906) and secretary of the Canadian Institute, Yeigh is best remembered for **"Canada's First Alpine Club Camp"** (1907)—the richest first-person narrative of the club's inaugural 1906 Yoho Pass camp. His work bridged mountaineering, cultural promotion, and Canadian geography during the nation's formative decades.

## The 1906 Yoho Camp & "Canada's First Alpine Club Camp"

### Publication
- **Title:** "Canada's First Alpine Club Camp"
- **Source:** *Canadian Alpine Journal*, Vol. 1, No. 1 (1907)
- **Author:** Frank Yeigh
- **Genre:** First-person narrative, mountaineering account
- **Historical Significance:** The definitive camp chronicle, capturing lived experience and organizational detail

### Content
Yeigh's account covers:
- **Camp life:** Daily routines, social dynamics of 100+ participants
- **Landscape:** Descriptions of Yoho Pass valley, "that striking picture of a tented town nestling amid the realm of trees"
- **Marches & expeditions:** Route to Emerald Lake, delta crossing
- **Signature climb:** Vice-President climb and mountain ceremonies
- **Photography:** Camp illustrated with photographs by Byron Harmon (official ACC photographer), Geo. R. B. Kinney, Frank W. Freeborn, and possibly Yeigh himself

### Access
- Full text: [Internet Archive (Canadian Alpine Journal 1907–8)](https://archive.org/stream/canadianalpinejo01alpiuoft/canadianalpinejo01alpiuoft_djvu.txt)
- Wikisource: [Canadian Alpine Journal/Volume 1/Number 1](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Canadian_Alpine_Journal/Volume_1/Number_1/Canada's_First_Alpine_Club_Camp)
